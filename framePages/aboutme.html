About Victoria

Greetings. My name is Victoria Vanderbach, and I love robots. For the longest time, I thought I would be a chemist like my aunt, but upon entering high school, I joined an organization that would change my life.

FIRST Robotics Competition

FIRST Robotics was my first encounter with hands on experience in programming and electronics. Through my three years participating on FIRST Robotics Team 1208 (Aug 2010 - May 2011) and Team 2068 (Aug 2011 - May 2013), I have learned two programming languages, how electronic systems work, and skills for interacting with mentors who are much more experienced than myself. All three years I participated in FIRST, both my teams took Gracious Professionalism awards at every competition. This award not only shows that a team is well organized and functioning, but that they also go above and beyond to impact their community and spread the message of FIRST. In my time in FIRST, I myself mentored a middle school robotics team, volunteered to run local robotics competitions, and ran demonstrations for local companies.

SuperNOVA - Zero Robotics Team

The Zero Robotics competition is by far the most amazing challenge I faced in my high school career. The challenge involved programming small satellites called SPHERES, and these satellites needed to run a task completely on its own. The tasks ranged from triangulating a position of an object with only the distance to the object, revolving around an unknown point, or avoiding the interactions of other SPHERES. For both years I was on the team, we made it to the final competition and witnessed our code operate on the International Space Station.

While at UIUC, I will be joining the Student Aircraft Builders. I will be on a team that will develop 'computer vision' to examine an airplane and look for imperfections that may not be visible to the human eye. I also hope to become involved in research, and I'm particularly interested in developing artificial intelligence. My hope is to find an internship or Co-op for the upcoming summer.

The End Goal

When all is said and done, I dream of being in a position where I can support FIRST Robotics, the organization that gave me so much and which I owe a lot to. Throughout high school, I've heard many girls my age say that they're interested in science and technology, but they don't think they're 'smart' enough to pursue it. I was once like that, but no more. FIRST showed me that hands-on experience is the best(and fasteset) way to learn new social, technical, and business skills. I discovered my passion for robotics and programming, and I transformed from a shy, socially awkward girl into a driven, vocal, and well-rounded individual. I am proud of who I am, and I'm proud to call myself a member of the FIRST organization. I wish to be able to volunteer at competitions and to mentor a team to inspire girls to pursue computer science as I am.
